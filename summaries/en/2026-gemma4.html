Gemma 4

A new generation of open-weight, natively multimodal models — dense (2.3B–31B) plus a 26B-A4B Mixture-of-Experts — engineered for compute efficiency and reasoning. The 2.3B-effective E2B out-reasons last year's Gemma 3 27B, and the 31B dense model becomes the leading dense open model on the LMArena leaderboard.

SECTION 01

Problem — Open, Multimodal, and Efficient at Once

Frontier capability keeps concentrating in closed, enormous models. Gemma 4's thesis is that you can ship open weights that are natively multimodal, strong at reasoning, and small enough to run on a phone — all in one family.

The open-weight community has faced a persistent trilemma: models are either (a) capable but closed, (b) open but text-only, or (c) open and multimodal but far behind on reasoning. Gemma 4 aims to collapse that trade-off by treating multimodality as native (not bolted-on), pushing reasoning through a "thinking mode," and co-designing every size for on-device deployment via quantization-aware training.

Native Multimodal

Text, vision, and audio in a single stack across every size — not a text model with adapters.

Reasoning Leap

Thinking traces before answers drive large STEM/coding gains; 31B tops open dense models on LMArena.

Runs Anywhere

E2B fits in <1 GB quantized; the family spans phone to single-GPU server.

Headline claim: The 2.3B-effective E2B beats Gemma 3 27B on reasoning benchmarks (AIME 2026, LiveCodeBench) despite roughly 10× fewer effective parameters — and the 31B dense model reaches Elo 1451 on LMArena, the leading dense open model at release (June 2026).

SECTION 02

The Model Lineup

Five checkpoints span two orders of magnitude: two "effective" small models built for on-device use, two mid/large dense models, and one sparse MoE that activates only a fraction of its weights.

"Effective" params (E2B/E4B) follow the Gemma 3n idea of a MatFormer-style nested model: a larger network (5B / 8B total) trained so that a smaller sub-network (2.3B / 4.5B "effective") can be sliced out for on-device inference. The 26B-A4B MoE holds 26B total parameters but routes each token through experts totaling only 3.8B activated — frontier-ish quality at small-model inference cost.

Read the two number columns carefully. "Total" is what you store; "Activated" is what runs per token. The MoE's appeal is a big gap between them (26B stored, 3.8B active); the E-models' appeal is the opposite trick — one checkpoint that can be shrunk at inference time.

SECTION 03

Architecture

A decoder-only Transformer with the now-standard Gemma stack — RMSNorm, QK-norm, RoPE, MQA-style KV sharing — plus interleaved local/global attention to keep the KV cache small, and lightweight native vision/audio encoders.

Attention: local-heavy interleaving

Gemma 4 interleaves local sliding-window and global attention layers at a ratio of 4:1 for E2B and 5:1 for the others — for every one global layer there are four or five cheap local ones. Global layers use pp-RoPE (p=0.25) with a base frequency of 1M; local layers use standard RoPE at 10k. Combined with KV-cache sharing across layer groups, the design cuts global KV-cache size by about 37.5% — the main lever for long-context memory footprint.

Fig 1 · Interleaved local/global attention block (schematic, per §Architecture)

Native encoders — vision & audio

Images pass through a SigLIP-style vision encoder (150M for E2B/E4B, 550M for 12B/31B) with patch size 16, emitting up to 1120 tokens at max resolution (with 70/140/280/560/1120 token tiers for cost/quality trade-offs). Audio uses a USM-based encoder (2 downsampling conv layers + 12 Conformer layers), shrunk to 305M — a 55% reduction from Gemma 3n's 680M. The 12B is "encoder-free": it ingests raw 40 ms audio chunks at 16 kHz (and image patches) directly through a projection, dropping the separate encoder entirely.

Tokenizer

A SentencePiece tokenizer with 262k entries — split digits, preserved whitespace, byte-level fallback — shared with the Gemini models, which keeps multilingual and code coverage strong.

SECTION 04

Training Recipe

Large-scale multimodal pre-training on TPU v5p/v6e, followed by a Gemma-3-style post-training pass that adds a "thinking mode," plus quantization-aware training so the released weights survive aggressive on-device quantization.

Multimodal pre-training

A large, diverse mixture of web documents, code, images and audio, with a January 2025 data cutoff. Data is filtered for personal information, unsafe content, and duplicates.

Post-training + thinking mode

Instruction tuning follows a similar approach to Gemma 3. The distinctive addition: models can emit a reasoning trace before answering, gated by control tokens (e.g. <|think|>) — the source of the large STEM/coding gains.

Quantization-Aware Training (QAT)

Models are trained to tolerate quantization: int2/int4 weights with int8 activations for mobile, and Q4_0 blockwise for desktop — so the small quantized checkpoints don't collapse in quality.

MTP drafter for speculative decoding

A multi-token-prediction drafter cross-attends to the main model's KV cache, accelerating inference via speculative decoding without a separate draft model.

SECTION 06

Efficiency & Deployment

Co-design for on-device use is the throughline: QAT-quantized checkpoints are small enough that E2B fits under a gigabyte, and the whole family scales from a phone to a single accelerator.

The on-device punchline: a QAT E2B checkpoint is 0.8 GB of weights plus a 0.05 GB KV cache — a reasoning-capable multimodal model that fits comfortably in phone RAM. At the top end, 31B quantizes from 64 GB (bf16) down to 19.2 GB, a single-GPU footprint.

SECTION 07

Limitations & Safety

The report is candid about what it doesn't disclose, and pairs the release with safety evaluations claimed to improve on Gemma 3.

Under-specified training details: total pre-training tokens are not stated, and post-training points back to Gemma 3 without naming the RL algorithm or thinking-mode training procedure — so exact reproduction from the report alone isn't possible.

Smaller-model degradation: gains from thinking mode are dramatic on reasoning, but the E-models still trail on broad knowledge (MMLU-Pro) and drop off sharply on 128k long-context retrieval (LOFT) versus the 31B.

Data cutoff: the January 2025 knowledge cutoff means the model has no native knowledge of events after that date.

Safety & responsibility

The team reports safety evaluations run without filters, targeting removal of CSAM, dangerous/explicit content, hate speech and harassment, plus filtering of personal and sensitive data. Their headline: Gemma 4 significantly outperforms Gemma 3 / 3n on safety metrics, framed as balancing openness against foreseeable risks. Models are released as open weights under Gemma's usage terms.

Why it matters: Gemma 4 is a data point that the open trilemma is softening — a phone-sized model can now reason, and a single-GPU dense model can lead a human-preference leaderboard among open releases. The interesting frontier it opens is thinking-mode-for-small-models: reasoning traces, not raw scale, produced most of the gains here.
